Founder Watchlist
April is Ocushield's best month of 2026 — net profit of £58.3k is a genuine turnaround from March's −£58k loss. The operational base is sound.
Eye Screening is now a critical revenue question. At £130.8k it represents 37% of April revenue. Is this a recurring B2B contract? One-off batch? May's P&L will be the real test.
YTD is £206k behind prior year on net profit. Primarily a revenue mix issue — the B2B / McKinsey revenue that drove H1 2025 has not been replaced in 2026.
Website DTC (UK + ROW) is declining structurally. Both channels are down ~20% MoM and 15–19% YoY. Digital marketing spend is also down 24% MoM — worth interrogating whether the pullback is causing the decline.
Amazon ROW is the standout DTC channel — up 51% MoM. The only channel showing consistent positive momentum. Worth understanding what's driving this and whether it can be amplified.
OpEx creep YoY is a structural concern. Total OpEx £85k vs £67k April '25 (+27%). Consulting has nearly doubled YoY; legal fees are elevated. YTD headcount costs also +124%.
Receivables of £178.5k need active management. This is almost equal to the cash balance. Stale receivables could mask a tighter cash position than the headline figure suggests.
