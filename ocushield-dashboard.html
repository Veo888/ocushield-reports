Performance diagnostic
Why is ROAS declining month on month?
Feb 1.79×
Mar 1.54× ↓
Apr 1.33× ↓
May MTD 1.56×
Root causes
1
Creative fatigue
CTR dropped from 0.83% in Feb/Mar to 0.69% in April — a clear signal your audience has seen the same ads too many times. ABO structures concentrate spend on a few ad sets, burning through audiences faster.
2
US campaigns dragging the average
Your US TOF campaigns run at 1.23× ROAS vs UK at 1.60×. If US spend share grew over this period, blended ROAS falls even if UK held steady.
3
Audience overlap & self-competition
Multiple overlapping UK/US TOF campaigns (4+ simultaneously) means you're bidding against yourself, inflating CPCs and saturating the same people faster.
4
Seasonality
Feb–Mar are historically strong for health/wellness. April sees a natural post-Q1 consumer spending dip in the UK. Check last year's data before over-attributing to campaign issues.
5
Attribution erosion
More broad/TOF activity means more view-through conversions in the mix. Meta's reported ROAS drifts downward as stronger last-click conversions shrink as a proportion of spend.
What to do
1
New creative immediately
Audit frequency across main campaigns — if above 3–4 in 30 days, you have a fatigue problem. Brief UGC, testimonial and educational formats, not just reskins.
2
Consolidate overlapping TOF campaigns
Merge 4 overlapping UK/US campaigns into fewer, better-funded ones. Reduces self-competition and gives the algorithm more signal to optimise.
3
Reallocate to DPA retargeting
DPA BOF campaigns are at 11.36× and 7.03× ROAS but spending <3% of budget. Shift 10–15% of budget from underperforming US campaigns here immediately.
4
Pause the two loss-makers
Stop cd-us-tof-asc-sales (0.55×) and US-TOF-Sales-Broad-ROAS (0.56×). Saves ~£800/month with negligible revenue impact.
5
Connect Shopify for true attribution
Meta-reported ROAS is self-attributed and inflated. You can't see true new vs returning customer revenue without your DTC backend connected.
