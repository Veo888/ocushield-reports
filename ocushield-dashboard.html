📘 Meta Ads diagnostic
ROAS declining — why & what to do
Feb1.79×
Mar1.54×↓
Apr1.33×↓
May MTD1.56×
Root causes
1
Creative fatigue
CTR dropped from 0.83% in Feb/Mar to 0.69% in April — a clear signal your audience has seen the same ads too many times. ABO structures concentrate spend on a few ad sets, burning through audiences faster.
2
US campaigns dragging the average
Your US TOF campaigns run at 1.23× ROAS vs UK at 1.60×. If US spend share grew over this period, blended ROAS falls even if UK held steady. This is further confirmed by Google data — your US Google campaigns also underperform UK equivalents.
3
Audience overlap & self-competition
4+ overlapping UK/US TOF campaigns running simultaneously means you're bidding against yourself, inflating CPCs and saturating the same people faster.
4
Seasonality
Feb–Mar are historically strong for health/wellness. April sees a natural post-Q1 consumer spending dip in the UK — and Google data shows the same volume drop, suggesting this is market-wide not campaign-specific.
5
Attribution erosion
More broad/TOF activity means more view-through conversions. Meta's reported ROAS drifts downward as last-click conversions shrink as a share of spend — and with Google also claiming the same clicks, true Meta ROAS is likely overstated.
What to do
1
New creative immediately
Audit frequency — if above 3–4 in 30 days, you have a fatigue problem. Brief UGC, testimonial and educational formats, not just reskins of existing ads.
2
Consolidate overlapping TOF campaigns
Merge 4 overlapping UK/US campaigns into fewer, better-funded ones. Reduces self-competition and gives the algorithm more signal to optimise with.
3
Reallocate to DPA retargeting
DPA BOF campaigns are at 11.36× and 7.03× ROAS but spending <3% of budget. Shift 10–15% from underperforming US campaigns here immediately.
4
Pause the two loss-makers
Stop cd-us-tof-asc-sales (0.55×) and US-TOF-Sales-Broad-ROAS (0.56×). Saves ~£800/month with negligible revenue impact given current conversion rates.
5
Fix US messaging — confirmed cross-channel
Both Meta US (1.23×) and Google US PMax (1.85×) underperform their UK equivalents. This points to a messaging or offer issue in the US market, not just a Meta problem. Review landing pages, pricing, and social proof for US audiences across both channels.

What's working
1
Brand search is printing money
UK-Search-Brand at 32.59× and US-Search-Brand at 17.30× ROAS. ROW brand terms also at 13–17×. These campaigns are highly efficient and protect brand equity — ensure budget is never capped here.
2
Search outperforms PMax for glasses
UK-Search-Glasses (2.22×) and UK-Search-Screens (2.42×) are outperforming equivalent PMax campaigns (UK-PMax-Glasses at 2.02×, UK-PMax at 2.00×). Search intent is strong for your product category.
3
Efficiency improving despite volume drop
ROAS went from 2.48× in Feb to 2.55× in April even as spend fell from £38K to £29K. The account is getting more efficient — a sign of good optimisation and audience quality improvement.
Watch & fix
1
US-PMax-zombie states
1.39× ROAS — the name is a red flag. If this campaign is targeting low-value US geographic segments, it's dragging the US Google average. Review and either exclude the worst-performing states or pause entirely.
2
EU-Search-Catch_All underperforming
1.41× ROAS on low volume. Consider pausing and redirecting budget to higher-performing UK/AUS campaigns, or restructuring with tighter keyword targeting for EU markets.
3
May MTD dip to 1.95× worth monitoring
The 3-month trend shows improvement but May MTD is lower at 1.95×. This may be early-month volatility — monitor through end of May before drawing conclusions.
4
Cross-channel conversion overlap
Google and Meta both claim credit for the same customer journeys. A user who clicks a Meta ad and later converts via Google search will appear in both platforms' reports. True combined ROAS is lower than the sum of the two.

Set minimum ROAS floor of 1.5×

Ring-fence brand search budget

Shift ROAS targets to contribution margin

Approve creative refresh budget
