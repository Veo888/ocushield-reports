Revenue fell £49,515 (−12.6%) from November's record £393k. Website UK dropped £20.7k (−18.1%) as Black Friday demand normalised. This is a predictable post-promotional pattern but the scale of drop reflects how much November was inflated by discounting.

B2B and other revenue fell from £57.5k in November to £29.4k in December (−48.9%). McKinsey revenue absent again. B2B Resellers also appears to have contracted. This gap directly drives the December net loss.

Marketing Projects — £10,109 ⚠ spike

Founder Watchlist
December is the first loss month of H2 2025 — net loss of £22.1k, a £32.6k swing from November's £10.5k profit. Sets the tone for a loss-making Q1 2026.
GP margin at 16.8% is the lowest recorded. Discounting costs have not normalised post-Black Friday while revenue has fallen sharply. This mismatch is the core P&L problem.
The December loss directly foreshadows 2026. The same pattern — absent McKinsey, declining DTC, structural discounting costs — persists through January, February and March 2026.
Amazon ROW is the one bright spot — £52.9k, up 21.6% YoY and stable MoM. This channel is the most resilient in the business.
Marketing Projects at £10.1k needs clarification — a 607% YoY increase. If this is a recurring agency commitment it adds ~£120k to annual OpEx. If one-off, no concern.
Outbound costs at £41.8k are anomalously high (+24.9% MoM on lower revenue). Likely relates to returns processing, seasonal carrier surcharges, or timing of shipments. Worth investigating.
